What is an annaprashan?

An annaprashan is a customary Hindu tradition marking a baby’s first feeding, or rice-feeding ceremony.

Annaprashan is a Sanskrit term, which means ‘grain initiation’.

In Bengal, the ceremony is referred to as “Mukhe Bhaat,” translating to “rice in your mouth.”

An annaprashan is usually celebrated on the 5th or 7th month for baby girls, and the 6th or 8th month for baby boys.

The annaprashan ritual is usually offered by the baby’s maternal uncle, otherwise known as Mama.
